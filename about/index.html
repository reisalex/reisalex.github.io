About

Alex Reis is a synthetic biologist and protein engineer with a background in biophysical modeling, multiplex CRISPR interference, phage display and antibody engineering. Alex is currently a Principal Scientist at Manifold Bio, a leading biotech startup in biologics discovery. With a background in chemical engineering and synthetic biology, Alex supports the innovation arms in both the wet and dry lab sides of the company. His work has driven the scaling and quantitative accuracy of Manifold’s proprietary protein barcoding tech, as well as other (currently unpublished) assays including Φ-seq and more.

Alex’s research approach leverages biophysical principles and practical biotechnological ‘wetware’ to drive business value through innovation. Through Alex’s rich research experiences over the past decade, he has recognized that interdisciplinary teams are what it takes to advance the biomedical sciences. Much of his time is spent mentoring younger scientists and research associates at Manifold, fostering a culture of innovation, growth and creativity.

In his free time, Alex games, rows, reads and works on creative projects. As a born and raised Texan, he is always looking for some legitimate tacos and Texas-style BBQ.
