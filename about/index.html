About

I’m a hybrid computational-experimental synthetic biology researcher, leveraging biophysical modeling and omics technologies to enable rational engineering of biology. I recently received my PhD under the mentorship of Dr. Howard Salis at Penn State University. My dissertation covered a variety of research topics including biophysical modeling of translation initation rate, nonrepetitive design of DNA, and scaled multiplex CRISPR interference. I’m a Texas native, graduated from Rice University with a BS in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering, and worked at Glycos Biotechnologies Inc., a metabolic engineering startup company using engineered E. coli to convert waste biomass into isoprene (synthetic rubber).

Read more about my projects , papers , and random musings on synthetic biology related news and research at my blog . Please feel free to reach out regarding anything! And please stay safe during COVID-19.
